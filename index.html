Hi from the maker of Éadaí!

What you're looking at here is a personal project I created because I wanted a way to discover and save the unique, high-quality, sustainable brands I liked — without being on Instagram.

Éadaí (pronounced "A-D") is Irish for "clothes," which is how this started. I was collecting clothing brands I loved from different pockets of the world I wouldn't usually come across.

Travelling to different cities, visiting local stores with big personalities and cool founders — no online shop can replace a brick and mortar. There's a sense of the brand's personality you really pick up in person, and it gives you so much more knowledge of the brand and attachment to the piece. It's an experience I don't want to see disappear.

So the ambition of this project is to solve a few problems I had. It's for people like me who love unique, well-made products; for smaller brands who need a sustainable, alternative way to be discovered by the people looking for them; and to help people find these stores when they travel.

This is an ongoing search for good materials, good values, good people. Every brand here is chosen personally by me. Éadaí wants these brands to thrive and takes no commission or money from them — this isn't an affiliate programme or a marketplace. Éadaí is independent, trying to build a sustainable way for these brands to get the business they need and deserve.

These brands are doing their best, but the retail ecosystem needs to work with them, not against them. The big companies are built for profit — they're not going to change it. But the consumer, the everyday person, can. Let's keep these brands — this inspiring, creative culture, this quality and craftsmanship — truly alive, and help them stand.

You'll also find some really lovely repair and alteration options, which are a big part of this — well-made things, and keeping and caring for them. Be sure to check them out. It's an area I notice is growing, so if you know of any, please paste their URL and I'll add it to Éadaí so people know about it.

This whole project is self-funded and independent — a side project I fit in around being a mum to a very tiny human and my professional job as a designer.

So please enjoy it, save the brands you like, and share it with anyone you think might appreciate it!

Thank you so much for taking the time to read this note, and thanks for giving Éadaí a go. I hope to make more good happen from this — but for now, this is a start.

Le gach dea-mhéin.
